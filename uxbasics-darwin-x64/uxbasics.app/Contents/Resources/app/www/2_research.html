RESEARCH

A basic market research can not only supplement user research but can also be used to
validate its findings at a later stage.

Step 1: Competitor Analysis

1. Conduct and document a user test of competing websites and apps

2. Look at current market trends

3. Look at price points for similar products


Download, signing up, purchasing and using products competing with yours is only part of the process;
conducting user testing sessions on these products will also yield valuable insights.

Step 2: Secondary Research

Step 3: Surveys

Crafting an online survey, primarily to solicit feedback from current (or potential) users.

Crafting a survey is easy; creating a good survey that asks questions in an unbiased way is not.
An effective survey can be a formidable tool for user research.

Step 4: Contextual Enquiry

You probably have an idea of what your user-base might look like.

Interview plausible users in the location that they are most likely to use the website or app, to understand their tasks and challenges.
