KEY DEFINITIONS

One of the most important key elements during an UX process is being able to define all aspects surrounding your product, whether it being the target audience, a customers journey through your product or even the concept itself.

Step 1: Personas

Personas are fictitious, specific, concrete representations of target users based on user research which will help you become more user focused and are an essential part of goal-directed design.

Creating personas for your project involves morphing qualitative and quantitative data from analyctics, surveys, interviews, user testing sessions, and other research activities into a handful of representative “typical” users/archetypes.

Important questions

1. Who are they?

2. What are their values?

3. What is their demography?

4. What needs do they have?

5. What are their goals?

Step 2: User Scenarios

A scenario is a narrative describing “a day in the life of” one of your personas, and probably includes how your website or app fits into their lives.

Step 3: Customer Journey Map

A CJM is a visual or graphic interpretation of the overall story from an individual’s perspective of their relationship with a organization, service, product or brand, over time and across channels.

Step 4: Define The Content

All the research is in place, you now define all the content your product will contain. If you’re going to have a text block with information, define what this block says in black and white. If you only have visuals, write down what they are visualizing, as it helps mapping out the flow of your communication.

Notes

1. Product content should reflect project goals and user’s needs

2. It should go hand-in-hand with the design of the product

3. It should be consistent, useful and accessible for users.


Keep in mind that some content will probably be changed during the process, and in some cases even removed.

Step 5: Test Your Concept & Content

When doing a concept/content test, you share the key essence of your product with a target audience, either one-on-one, or in larger groups. This can be done either in person or online through a survey.
